About me

I am a physician and researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and medical image analysis, with a particular interest in deep learning for clinical applications. My background combines medical training with experience in computational pathology, and I am increasingly focused on multimodal foundation models and their potential across different medical imaging domains.

I received my medical degree from Goethe University Frankfurt am Main in 2024. My medical doctoral thesis, supervised by Prof. Peter J. Wild and Prof. Jochen Triesch, was conducted jointly at Dr. Senckenberg Institute for Pathology (SIP) and Frankfurt Institute for Advanced Studies (FIAS). The project focused on predicting and characterizing molecular subtypes of muscle-invasive bladder cancer using deep learning methods, including foundation models and attention-based multiple instance learning. The thesis is currently undergoing final minor corrections prior to submission and defense.
